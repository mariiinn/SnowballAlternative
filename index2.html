The sleep cycle

Our sleep cycle has five stages that repeats about every 90 minutes through the night. That is four NREM (Non-rapid eye movement) stages and a REM (Rapid eye movement) stage.

N1 The "dozing off" stage. Breathing rate slows, but the body isn’t fully relaxed yet.
N2 Body temperature drops, muscles relax, breathing becomes regular and eye movement stops. A person usually spends around half their sleep in N2 sleep.
N3/N4 Also called deep sleep. The body relaxes even further. It is believed that the body restore health and growth in this stage.
REM Breathing becomes irregular, the heart beats faster and the eyes moves. At this time the body experiences temporary paralysis which means you wouldn't actually start running if you dream about running. Though there is possible to dream in earlier stages, the dreams accumulated in this stage are more vivid and common.

Around a quarter of the night is spent in REM sleep. A person spends nearly 600 hours dreaming each year during REM sleep. It is believed you can learn in your dreams, strengthen memory and creativity. In a busy world where we never have enough time, we want to exploit the hours spent in REM sleep to expand horizons.
